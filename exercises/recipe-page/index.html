PIZZA ROLLS - TOTINO'S COPY CAT

These pizza rolls were a huge part of my childhood — my mom always had a stash ready for school lunches or as an after-school snack. Think of them as a homemade Totinos copycat, but way better. Theyre piping hot, cheesy, and ooze like lava the second you take a bite.

     PREP TIME: 30 MINUTES      COOK TIME: 15 MINUTES

     TOTAL TIME: 45 MINUTES       YIELDS: 5 TO 6

INGREDIENTS

FILLING

  • 1 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 3/4 cup pizza sauce
  • 1/2 cup chopped turkey pepperoni
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 cup grated parmesan
  • 1 tablespoon chopped basil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley

FOR ASSEMBLY

  • 25 wonton wrappers
  • vegetable oil, for frying

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Make the filling: In a bowl, mix together the shredded mozzarella, pizza sauce, chopped turkey pepperoni, red pepper flakes, oregano, parmesan, basil, and parsley until everything is well combined. Set aside.
  2. Stuff and assemble: Have a small bowl of water ready for sealing. Lay out the wonton wrappers in a diamond shape so the corners point up, down, left, and right.
  3. Spoon a small dollop of filling (about 1 to 1 ½ teaspoons) into the center of each wrapper.
  4. Dip your finger in water and run it along all the edges of the wrapper.
  5. Fold the bottom corner up over the filling to form a triangle.
  6. Fold in the left and right corners toward the center.
  7. Then fold the top corner down and press to seal everything shut like an envelope. Make sure all the edges are tightly sealed so no filling leaks out.
  8. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C). Once hot, carefully add the rolls in batches and fry until golden brown and crispy on all sides, about 2 to 3 minutes. Remove and place on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  9. Serve hot with extra pizza sauce or ranch for dipping!

TIPS & NOTES

Q: Can I make these ahead of time?

A: Yes! You can assemble the rolls, cover them tightly in a ziploc bag, and refrigerate them for up to a day before baking. You can also freeze them!

Q: Can I freeze pizza rolls?

A: Absolutely. Once rolled up, freeze them in a single layer on a tray, then transfer to a freezer bag. Then just fry as usual straight from the freezer or bake in an air fryer or oven.

Q: How do I make these vegetarian?

A: Skip the pepperoni or any meat and load them up with your favorite veggies instead! Mushrooms, bell peppers, onions, olives, or spinach all work great.

Q: How do I keep the rolls from unrolling while baking?

A: Seal the edges well by pinching or brushing a little water along the seam before rolling. Freezing also helps them seal up better. If you choose to air fry or bake, cooking them seam-side down also helps lock everything in.

Q: Can I air fry pizza rolls instead of baking?

A: Yes! They get super crispy in the air fryer. Cook them at 375°F for 8–10 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden brown. Just make sure to spray well with oil before air frying.

Q: How do I reheat leftovers?

A: Pop them back in the oven or air fryer for a few minutes so they crisp up again. The microwave works in a pinch, but they won’t be as crunchy.
